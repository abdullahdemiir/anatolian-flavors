Appetizers

Stuffed Grape Leaves

Grape leaves stuffed with rice, served with lemon. This dish, known as "Dolma" in Turkish, has deep roots in Anatolian cuisine, symbolizing the region's tradition of using fresh, local ingredients. Dolma represents the shared heritage of the many cultures that have influenced Turkish cuisine over the centuries.

Eggplant Puree

This dish, often referred to as "Baba Ghanoush," is a staple in Anatolian and Middle Eastern cuisines. The smoky flavor of roasted eggplant is enhanced with garlic, lemon, and tahini, making it a beloved appetizer that reflects the diverse flavors of the region.

Hummus

Hummus, a creamy blend of chickpeas, tahini, olive oil, and lemon juice, is not just a dish but a cultural symbol of hospitality in Anatolia. It is commonly served as a dip or spread, embodying the simplicity and richness of the region's culinary traditions.

Spicy Tomato Dip

Known locally as "Ezme," this spicy tomato dip is a vibrant mix of tomatoes, peppers, and herbs. It is often served alongside kebabs and grilled meats, adding a fresh and tangy flavor that complements the smoky richness of Anatolian barbecue.

Soups

Lentil Soup

Lentil Soup, or "Mercimek Çorbası," is a cornerstone of Turkish cuisine, often enjoyed as a comforting start to meals. Its simple ingredients—lentils, onions, and spices—reflect the frugality and warmth of Anatolian cooking.

Tarhana Soup

Tarhana Soup is a traditional Turkish soup made from fermented and dried yogurt and grain. It is a symbol of Anatolian ingenuity, turning simple ingredients into a nutritious and hearty meal that has sustained generations.

Yogurt Soup

This light and refreshing soup, known as "Yayla Çorbası," combines yogurt, rice, and mint, embodying the pastoral roots of Anatolian culture. It is a dish that highlights the importance of dairy in the region's diet.

Ezogelin Soup

Named after the legendary bride Ezo, this soup is made from red lentils, bulgur, and rice. It is a dish steeped in folklore, symbolizing the resilience and resourcefulness of Turkish women.

Main Courses

Iskender Kebab

Iskender Kebab, originating from the city of Bursa, is one of the most famous dishes in Turkey. It consists of thinly sliced döner meat served over pide bread, topped with yogurt, tomato sauce, and melted butter, representing the indulgent side of Turkish cuisine.

Manti

Manti are Turkish dumplings typically filled with spiced meat and served with yogurt and garlic sauce. This dish reflects the Central Asian roots of Turkish cuisine, blending flavors and techniques passed down through generations.

Sultan's Delight

This dish, known as "Hünkar Beğendi," is made of grilled lamb served on a bed of creamy, smoky eggplant puree. It dates back to the Ottoman Empire and is a dish fit for royalty, showcasing the luxurious side of Anatolian cooking.

Stewed Beans with Meat

Stewed Beans, or "Kuru Fasulye," is often called the national dish of Turkey. This simple yet satisfying dish is a testament to the importance of legumes in the Anatolian diet, often served with rice pilaf.

Desserts

Baklava

Baklava is a rich, sweet pastry made of layers of filo dough filled with chopped nuts and sweetened with syrup. It is perhaps the most iconic dessert of the Ottoman Empire, representing the opulence and refinement of Turkish desserts.

Rice Pudding

Rice Pudding, or "Sütlaç," is a creamy dessert that is often baked to achieve a caramelized top layer. It is a beloved comfort food in Turkey, reflecting the country's deep connection to dairy and rice.

Künefe

Künefe is a dessert made with shredded phyllo dough, filled with cheese, and soaked in sweet syrup. Originating from the Middle East, it is a favorite in southern Turkey, known for its unique combination of textures and flavors.

Revani

Revani is a semolina cake soaked in sweet syrup, often flavored with lemon or orange. This dessert is a classic in Turkish cuisine, offering a light and airy sweetness that is perfect after a hearty meal.

Beverages

Ayran

Ayran is a traditional Turkish yogurt drink that is both refreshing and hydrating. It is a staple at Turkish tables, especially during the hot summer months, reflecting the pastoral traditions of Anatolia.

Turnip Juice

Turnip Juice, or "Şalgam," is a fermented drink made from purple carrots and turnips, often enjoyed alongside kebabs. It is a popular beverage in southern Turkey, known for its tangy and slightly spicy flavor.

Turkish Coffee

Turkish Coffee is more than just a drink; it is an integral part of Turkish culture. Brewed from finely ground coffee beans, it is known for its strong flavor and is traditionally served in small cups with a glass of water.

Brewing Tea

Turkish Tea, or "Çay," is a fundamental part of daily life in Turkey. It is brewed in a special double teapot and served in small tulip-shaped glasses, symbolizing hospitality and warmth in Turkish culture.

Salads

Shepherd's Salad

Shepherd's Salad, or "Çoban Salatası," is a fresh, crisp salad made with tomatoes, cucumbers, onions, and green peppers, topped with olive oil and lemon juice. It is a perfect accompaniment to grilled meats and is a staple in Turkish cuisine.

Eggplant Salad

This smoky and tangy eggplant salad, known as "Patlıcan Salatası," is made from roasted eggplants, garlic, lemon, and olive oil. It's a favorite in the summer months and a common side dish in Turkey.

Gavurdağı Salad

Gavurdağı Salad is a unique Turkish salad that combines finely chopped vegetables with walnuts, pomegranate syrup, and a spicy dressing. It's known for its bold flavors and crunchy texture.

Purslane Salad

Purslane Salad, or "Semizotu Salatası," is a light and refreshing salad made from purslane, yogurt, garlic, and olive oil. It is both nutritious and delicious, showcasing the simplicity of Turkish cuisine.

Side Dishes

Bulghur Pilaf

Bulghur Pilaf, or "Bulgur Pilavı," is a hearty and healthy side dish made from bulgur wheat, onions, and tomatoes. It is a common accompaniment to meat dishes in Turkey and is valued for its nutritional benefits.

Fried Eggplant and Peppers

This dish, known as "Kızartma," is a simple yet flavorful side dish of fried eggplant and green peppers, typically served with a garlic-yogurt sauce. It is a popular summertime treat in Turkey.

Stuffed Peppers

Stuffed Peppers, or "Biber Dolması," are bell peppers stuffed with a mixture of rice, pine nuts, and currants. This dish can be served warm or cold and is a beloved part of Turkish home cooking.

Grilled Vegetables

Grilled Vegetables, or "Izgara Sebzeler," are a healthy and delicious side dish featuring seasonal vegetables like zucchini, tomatoes, and peppers. Grilled to perfection, they complement a wide range of main courses.
